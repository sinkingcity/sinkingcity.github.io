Ode to Pablo Picasso by Daniel Ruiz

“In the Spanish way he was Pablo Picasso y Ruiz

and some of his early canvases are signed Pablo

Ruiz but of course Pablo Picasso was the better name.”

-Gertrude Stein


My grandfather swearswe’re related,

that you’re hiseighth cousin, but no one knows


how many arms you have,

and every timeI see Les Demoiselles d’Avignon


I grow an eye without having to water it.Do you know

how muchblood is in the body?


Dada, Daddy, eldestcousin, fellow

artist of my family,you slicedthe body into squares

and sold them.Tell me what art is


because I don’t know. I’ve been told that spider

webs are the strongestmaterials in the world,


but you say“Art is a lie that makes us realize the truth.”

Tell me what’s stronger. Show me what


a facelooks like.I never learned

to play the guitar—

shall I try the mandolin?


You know what is true is not what is correct.

You know that art talks about itby not talking about it.


With long enough legs, I could step over

the Great Wall

like a Snickers wrapper


tossed

on the sidewalk. Call it a Readymade—

that trash I drop

from my pocketswhich you assemble,


myblood,myambiguous octopus,


if I could borrow an arm.


At MoMA,


you have your bedroom

and every day is anall day orgy

of flashless photos.


Pablo Picasso y Ruiz,

I am alwaysfinding you,


the trail of blood fading in the sidewalk,


in the streets, under

a sewage plate,

or at McDonalds

paying in pennies only.


I know you’re in hiding.

I know my grandfather is right about you.


I know I know nothing,

but I’m not sure.


Daniel Eduardo Ruiz was born in Bayamón, Puerto Rico, but currently lives in Valparaíso, Chile on a Fulbright Scholarship. His poems can or will be found in The Journal, Harpur Palate, Minnesota review, The Adroit Journal, New Delta Review, and elsewhere.
